A read on where AI actually fits in FYELABS' stack, and where it doesn't.

You spun out of McMaster in 2021, you have run over 200 fixed-price builds, and you pitch hardware, software, and AI under one roof. The testimonials page names twenty clients. XRAI Glass, Vita Innovations, ViaCyte, BandConnect, GEOSOURCE. None of them lead with the AI story. That is not a criticism. It is a pattern. Shops that do real product work know the AI piece is often the smallest part of the build, and the part clients most overspecify.

The frame

Roughly 60 / 30 / 10.

Our working view on any stack is roughly 60 / 30 / 10. Sixty percent of what feels like an AI problem is a database, a queue, and a well-formed SQL query. Thirty percent is rule-based logic that a domain expert could write in a weekend. Ten percent is genuine machine learning: sensor fusion on a wearable, computer vision on a medical device, anomaly detection on industrial telemetry. The projects on your testimonials wall look like projects where that ratio is exactly right, and the ML is doing its job without announcing itself.

Where this matters for a fixed-price shop is earlier. When a founder walks in asking for "an AI feature," the five minutes spent deciding which layer that feature belongs on is the difference between a profitable milestone and a scope-creep writeoff. We have spent the last eighteen months building the methodology around that decision. A lot of it was published as a paper this year.

The proof

A partnership that already works.

We run a partnership with NLP Logix for work that sits below the orchestration layer. Model training at production scale, data platform work, the heavy build lift. NLP Logix has been in machine learning since 2011 and runs over 150 data scientists. We hold the strategy and orchestration layer, they hold the build layer, both sides send overflow, both sides get paid. The pattern works because the split is clean: we decide what belongs where, they ship what lives below it. A similar pattern could work between Eduba and FYELABS in the opposite direction, with Eduba holding US-side strategy and orchestration for clients who need your build team underneath.

For the peer-services credibility question, Eduba's founder Jake Van Clief also ran a consulting engagement with KPMG UK, one of the Big Four. Forty-plus executives, regulated industry, methodology delivered. That is the conversation we expect you to want first, before any discussion of joint delivery.
